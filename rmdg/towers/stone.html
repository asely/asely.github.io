St. Mary the Virgin, Stone-in-Oxney

Details of the Bells

Treble 5-2-0 F 1788 Wm. & Thos. Mears I
2nd 6-0-0 E 1786 Wm. Mears
3rd 7-0-0 Db c1450 John Danyell of London
4th 8-0-0 C 1795 Thos. Mears I
5th 9-2-0 Bb 1787 Wm. & Thos. Mears I
Tenor 18-0-0 Ab 1787 Wm. & Thos. Mears I

History

c1450 Five bells cast by John Danyell.
1720 Treble recast
1786 Treble recast by William Mears.
1787 Four and Tenor recast by William & Thomas Mears I.
1788 Treble added to make ring of six.
1795 Fourth recast by Thomas Mears I.
1880's Bells restored and rehung.  A local company were instrumental in bringing this project to fruition.  This local company included a Mr. Frank Wenham of Stone Green Farm, Stone.
1913 June 22nd - Mr. Frank Wenham passed away.  His sons, Wilfred and Cyril were ringers at this time, however, along with Alfred Daw and Charles W. Player.  They were all active members of the RM&DG and the tower figured prominently in the Guild's activities.  All these men took part in peals of Grandsire Triples at towers including New Romney, Appledore, Rye and other eight bell towers in the area.  There were also a large number of less active members of the local band.
1934 Five and Tenor rehung on ball bearings.  Front four rehung on the existing plain bearings, having cleaned the gudgeons and bearings and rebushed them.  All other working parts of installation overhauled and strengthening added to frame.
1960's There was quite an active local band.  However, when the long term incumbent retired, some mischievous persons claimed that the bells were in a ruinous condition and should not be rung.

The old band at Stone in Oxney.  Mr Frank Wenham (current Captain) is pictured back row 2nd from right.  Alf Daw (who taught him to ring) was a very active member of the original  RM & DG.  He is sitting in the front row on the extreme left.
1980 Mr. Frank Wenham, son of Cyril Wenham put the tower into order and began to organise ringing on the bells once more.
1988 December 24th - A peal of Doubles was rung, conducted by Crawford T. Hillis.  This included two members directly descended from Frank Wenham I (grandfather of Frank Wenham - the other Wenham was not related.)  This peal disproved the theory that the bells were unringable.  Mr. Frank Wenham is tower captain and ensures that the bells are rung for the monthly Sunday evensong.
2001 Mr. Wenham repaired the roller which fell off the fifth bell while a visiting band were ringing there.  At an RM & DG practice Mr. Wenham was elected a member of the Guild, and members unanimously agreed that it was a privilege to have Mr. Wenham as a member of the Guild; his family having been connected with Stone bells for so long - his father being a Guild member - and his unstinting perseverance in maintaining the bells and saving them from the tuning machine and the hated modern fittings!
2005 The KCACR bell restoration team carried out work on tightening the framework, which considerably improved the 'go' of the bells, and cut down the amount of movement in the going parts.
