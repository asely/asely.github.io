Michaelmas 2005

My dear people,
                            Following a wonderful summer season, we once again find ourselves back struggling against the dark elements within our beloved church.

In late August, I attended Evensong here at St Mary’s.  This was led by the new Curate, who began the service late.  The fluency was at best lacking, as the service wound along its weary way, and then I was publicly admonished for simply pointing out that there is only one Office Hymn – the second LESSON (NOT Reading!) is followed immediately by the Nunc Dimittis, not another Hymn.  I’m sure that the fellow is on a learning curve, but frankly it is scandalous that anyone is being ordained without a fluent and intimate grasp of the doctrine and language of our incomparable 1662 Book of Common Prayer.  No doubt the Curate had also been briefed about what a troublemaker I am (because I fight the modernising lunacy – but trying to have a stand-up row with one of the congregation for gently advising the correct procedure at Evensong is just despicable.  It’s the world on its head, isn’t it?  The one who defends our religion most staunchly is despised and pilloried.  Not just me, but all staunch Traditionalists.  We are not allowed a view, so say the totalitarian liberal elite.  Out and out Evangelicals who are prepared to go 50/50 with either all out services of Praise & Worship we can contend with.  They give us our BCP –unadulterated – and then they do their stuff separately.  They want to help; they want to find a solution to problems.  The bullies around here only seek to drive us out.  We aren’t getting the true BCP – what we are getting is services which are doctored according to the whim of whoever happens to be ‘leading’ them.  That means that the all important Ten Commandments are omitted from the 8am Holy Communion (1662), and the ridiculous political correctness of inserting “mankind”, “humankind”, “all people” or some other crass idiocy instead of just understanding that “all men” encompasses both sexes.  It always did, and it always will.  Likewise we have to use the readings for the Vth Form remove at Evensong, rather than the proper Authorised Version (this tinkering is very common these days) and extempore prayers which are so ghastly and trite that words just defeat me at this point!  If only this generation of Clergy would understand the basics of masculine and feminine in Latin, but then we don’t have many Clergy now with MA (Cantab) or MA (Oxon) after their name and it shows in the sort of mind-numbing trash which they force us to endure, and pass off as worship!

I feel that we really are at the lowest ebb ever though, when people are playing political games in the middle of a service, and abusing the power conveyed upon them by Bishops to silence those who are of a right conversation.  Could Canon Sharp ever have possibly imagined that 33 years after his retirement this level of un-Christian bullying might prevail?  This is often a question which I ponder in my mind; not just with regard to St Mary - the – Virgin, Ashford but at St Nicholas Leeds - the wife of the last BCP-adhering Vicar there confirmed to me that her husband would have been absolutely appalled at the abuse that the current Priest in Charge there, Professor Robin Gill (a modern Theologist – there’s a contradiction! – at University of Kent) continues to level at me.  “It would not have happened when John was Vicar”, Canon John Robinson’s wife assured me just a few months ago.

So much for the “no support for my views” which people of the ilk of Colin Preece or Robin Gill vitriolically assure me.  Both have told me in no uncertain terms to “get out” of their respective Parishes.  Their Parish?  I wonder what God thinks of that statement.  Neither of these two individuals (nor many others it would appear) have ever studied the BCP Ordination service.  Here’s a question which the Bishop is supposed to ask of each potential Priest at the Ordination service: (Direct Quote from 1662 BCP) “Will you reverently obey your Ordinary and other chief Ministers, unto whom is committed the charge and government over you; following with a glad mind and will their godly admonitions, and
 *For the avoidance of doubt the word ‘man’ here includes all people, as does every reference of its kind in the 1662 BCP.
submitting yourselves to their godly judgements?”  I think Messrs Gill, Preece and our new Curate might have a problem with the response “I will so do, the Lord being my helper” to this question!  Modern Clergy need to ‘get real’ and work out that they are NOT ‘the boss’.  It’s a step down being a Minister, NOT a step up!  Until they do they will only attract spineless fellow travellers on the crusade to bully every traditionalist into outer darkness.  They might think that’s a great idea, but the spineless are only seen in the fair weather.  They will soon ‘bail out’ when the money dries up – and that will happen to the whole Church of England in just a few years – its outdated spendthrift ways are unsustainable.

Our website will be up and running soon, and I do exhort all of you with computer access to view it.  Copies of this letter may be printed from it, and distributed.  However, I will always ensure that all those of you without this ‘new-fangled’ equipment are provided with copies!

Once again many thanks to you all for your continuing support in these dark days.  There are a great many perils and dangers still facing us who defend and campaign for real religion, but surely we can face these in sure confidence that the Prayer Book is our shield – our knowledge is our greatest weapon against complacency, and against the modern liturgy of Common Worship which teaches nothing, and thus causes ignorance of the disciplines and tenets of our faith as revealed in the Creeds, Ten Commandments and 39 Articles of Religion to grow.
